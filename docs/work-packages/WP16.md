## Mathematical role

For positive-definite `R`, define the density ratio relative to product standard Gaussian measure
by

```text
L_R(x) = (sqrt (det R))⁻¹ exp (-(qform (R⁻¹ - I) x) / 2).
```

This package proves the exact with-density measure identity and a positive minimum of `L_R` on a
centered coordinate box. Independently, WP15's weak convergence implies convergence of each
iterated scalar law on `[-L,L]`, since the nondegenerate Gaussian limit gives zero mass to the two
boundary points. The finite product of these box masses therefore has an eventual positive lower
bound. Integrating the density-ratio minimum against the product factors yields one real constant
that eventually bounds every correlated dyadic product integral from below.

No unit-diagonal or log-concavity assumption belongs in this package. Those hypotheses are needed
by the WP14 deficit step, not by the density/CLT lower bound. The theorem also handles `m = 0`
without a separate branch.

Relevant plan sections are §§4.2, 4.8, 6.2, 7, 10, 12 (WP16), 13, 14, and 17. The relevant
mathematical source section is §6.3 of `notes/simplex_optimality_proof.v3.md`.

## Proof architecture

1. Express product standard Gaussian measure as volume with its finite-coordinate density.
2. Transport volume through the inverse positive-definite matrix square root and cancel the
   everywhere-positive standard density to obtain the exact ratio formula.
3. Take the positive minimum of the continuous ratio on a compact centered coordinate box.
4. Use the ratio measure identity and finite-product Fubini to integrate the pointwise box minimum
   against arbitrary nonnegative bounded factors.
5. Apply Portmanteau to WP15's varying-source convergence. Positive variance makes the Gaussian
   interval boundary null and the interval mass strictly positive.
6. Take the finite product of scalar box masses, convert it from ENNReal measure values to real set
   integrals, and combine it with the fixed density minimum.
