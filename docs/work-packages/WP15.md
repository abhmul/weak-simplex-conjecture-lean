## Mathematical role

Define `D⁰h = h` and `Dʳ⁺¹h = D(Dʳh)`. This package proves that all analytic and moment
properties needed for repeated application of WP14 propagate through `Dʳ`. It then identifies
`Dʳh · dγ` with the law of

```text
(2^r)⁻¹⁄² ∑ k < 2^r, Y_k
```

for i.i.d. `Y_k` with law `h · dγ`. Boundedness relative to the Gaussian supplies a second
moment, zero Gaussian barycenter supplies centering, and the pinned one-dimensional CLT composed
with `r ↦ 2^r` yields convergence of the actual iterated density measures. Their common variance
is strictly positive because an absolutely continuous measure over a non-atomic Gaussian cannot be
a point mass.

Relevant plan sections are §§4.2, 4.5, 4.7, 6.2, 7, 10, 12 (WP15), 13, 14, and 17. The
relevant mathematical source sections are §§6.1 and 6.3 of
`notes/simplex_optimality_proof.v3.md`.

WP15 depends directly on WP13, not WP14. WP14 consumes the same transform in the deficit inequality;
WP17 combines the two completed interfaces.

## Proof architecture

1. Propagate measurability, nonnegativity, boundedness, log-concavity, mass, barycenter, and
   variance by induction using WP13.
2. Recursively identify normalized averaging with a balanced convolution power scaled by
   `(sqrt 2)⁻ʳ` and prove its characteristic-function power formula.
3. Use independence to identify the sum of the first `2 ^ r` coordinates with that convolution
   power, giving the exact `HasLaw` theorem.
4. On `ℝ^ℕ` with `Measure.infinitePi`, apply mathlib's centered CLT and compose its limit with
   `r ↦ 2 ^ r`.
5. Transfer the dyadic random-variable convergence through the exact law to the varying density
   measures.
6. Separately derive positive variance from non-atomicity and the variance-zero AE-constant theorem.

## Forbidden shortcuts

- Do not assume nonzero variance; derive strict positivity from the stated density hypotheses.
- Do not replace `2 ^ r` by an asymptotically equivalent sequence or leave the normalization
  algebra informal.
- Do not assume probability instances for the iterated density measures; derive them from mass
  preservation.
- Do not expose private convolution powers or characteristic-function plumbing.
- Do not weaken direct convergence of the iterated density measures to only the raw product-space
  CLT.
- Do not add continuity, strict positivity of `h`, higher moments, external code, placeholders,
