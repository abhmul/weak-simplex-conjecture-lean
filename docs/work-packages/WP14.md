## Mathematical role

For independent `X, X' ∼ N(0, R)`, the normalized sum and difference

```text
U = (X + X') / √2,    V = (X - X') / √2
```

have joint law `N(0, R) ⊗ N(0, R)`. For fixed `U = u`, each coordinate factor in `V` is
measurable, nonnegative, bounded, even, and log-concave. WP12 therefore compares its correlated
integral with the product of its standard Gaussian marginals, which are precisely the WP13
normalized self-convolutions. Product Fubini and the joint Gaussian rotation law then give

```text
Z_R(D h) ≤ Z_R(h)^2.
```

No mass-one, zero-barycenter, or variance assumption belongs in this package. Those hypotheses first
enter the dyadic CLT iteration and the final contradiction.

Relevant plan sections are §§4.2, 4.6, 6.2, 7, 10, 12 (WP14), 13, 14, and 17. The relevant
mathematical source section is §6.2 of `notes/simplex_optimality_proof.v3.md`.

## Proof architecture

1. Express the normalized sum–difference map as a `π / 4` continuous-linear rotation after
   `Prod.swap`, and use centered Gaussian rotation invariance.
2. For fixed `u`, define
   `v ↦ h ((u + v) / √2) * h ((u - v) / √2)` and prove its measurability,
   nonnegativity, boundedness, evenness, and log-concavity.
3. Identify its standard Gaussian integral with `normalizedSelfConvolution h u` by an audited
   real/ENNReal bridge.
4. Apply WP12 pointwise in `u`, then integrate the inequality using bounded-product
   integrability.
5. Use Gaussian rotation invariance and product Fubini to identify the double integral with
   `Z_R(h)^2`.
