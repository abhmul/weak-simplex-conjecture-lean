## Mathematical role

This package proves the positive-definite finite-radius symmetric Gaussian rectangle inequality
needed by the even-factor layer-cake theorem. The proof uses Gaussian regression without regular
conditional distributions, Prékopa log-concavity of the shifted residual mass, a narrow
one-dimensional threshold covariance inequality, and dimension induction. Singular covariance is
not handled here; the product branch needs only the positive-definite theorem.

Relevant plan sections are §§3.1, 4.2–4.4, 6.2, 7, 10, 12 (WP11), 13, 14, and 17. Relevant
mathematical source sections are §§2.3 and 5 of `notes/simplex_optimality_proof.v3.md`.

## Proof architecture

1. Define the residual `Yᵢ = Xᵢ - Rᵢ,last X_last` and prove it independent of the last coordinate by
   Gaussianity and exact covariance cancellation.
2. Represent the residual law as a linear image of a full product standard Gaussian, so its shifted
   rectangle mass is measurable, even, and log-concave by WP10 Prékopa.
3. Rewrite the full rectangle as the shifted-mass integral restricted to the last coordinate's
   centered interval; rewrite the unrestricted integral as the principal-marginal rectangle.
4. Prove an even log-concave ENNReal function antitone on `[0,∞)` and apply the exact threshold
   restricted-lintegral inequality.
5. Induct on `Fin m`; use the positive-definite principal submatrix and
   `Fin.prod_univ_castSucc` in the successor case.
