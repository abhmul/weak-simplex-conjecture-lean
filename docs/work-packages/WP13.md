## Mathematical role

This package realizes the density-coordinate normalized average

```text
(D h)(u) = ∫ h((u + v) / √2) h((u - v) / √2) dγ(v).
```

It proves the analytic closure properties consumed by WP14 and the exact law identity consumed by
WP15. Mass one, zero Gaussian barycenter, and variance are then derived from that law. An arbitrary
mean is not preserved: normalized averaging multiplies it by `√2`; only zero barycenter is
preserved.

Relevant plan sections are §§4.2, 4.5, 6.2, 7, 10, 12 (WP13), 13, 14, and 17. The relevant
mathematical source section is §6.1 of `notes/simplex_optimality_proof.v3.md`.

The reassessment dependency diagram places WP12 before WP13, but the measured theorem dependency
for this file is WP10 only. WP12 first enters the sum--difference argument in WP14. This package may
therefore be productionized in parallel with WP12 without changing any theorem statement or
mathematical branch.

## Proof architecture

1. Define an internal ENNReal transform and expose its `toReal` wrapper.
2. Prove measurability, nonnegativity, and the explicit square bound.
3. Multiply the two affine factor pullbacks by the standard Gaussian density and apply the WP10
   Prékopa theorem through a private `Fin 1` marginalization wrapper.
4. Rotate a product of two standard Gaussian measures by `π / 4`; mathlib's convention sends the
   first coordinate to `(y + y') / √2`.
5. Combine rotation invariance, product Fubini, and `prod_withDensity` to prove the exact map law.
6. Derive mass one, zero barycenter, and variance preservation from that law. Do not recompute the
   moments from the defining double integral.
