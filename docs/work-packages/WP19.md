## Mathematical role

For a scalar `ε ∈ (0,1)`, set

```text
Rε = (1 - ε) • R + ε • 1.
```

The matrix `Rε` remains a weak-simplex covariance and is positive definite. WP18 therefore proves
the lower-orthant inequality for every `Rε`. Couple a Gaussian with covariance `R` and an
independent standard Gaussian so that

```text
Xε = sqrt (1 - ε) • X + sqrt ε • Z
```

has covariance `Rε` and converges almost surely to `X` as `ε → 0`. The frontier of the equal-level
lower orthant is contained in the finite union of coordinate hyperplanes `{x | x i = c}`. Every
coordinate law is `gaussianReal 0 1`, so this frontier is null. Portmanteau then passes the WP18
inequality to the singular limit.

This handles singular covariance exactly once and closes M3. It does not extend the centered
product theorem to singular matrices. Relevant plan sections are §§1.4, 3.9, 4.1, 6.2, 7, 10,
11.6, 12 (WP19), 13–14, and 18. The relevant mathematical source section is §10 of
`notes/simplex_optimality_proof.v3.md`.
