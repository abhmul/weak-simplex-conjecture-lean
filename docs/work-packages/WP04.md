## Strict-positivity proof

Use the unnormalized conditioned-variance numerator:

```lean
Ōł½ x in Set.Iic s, (x + r s) ^ 2 * normalPDF x =
  normalCDF s * (1 - s * r s - (r s) ^ 2)
```

The left side is strictly positive because its integrand is nonnegative and vanishes only at `{-r s}`; remove that null singleton from the positive support. Since `normalCDF s > 0`, ordered-field algebra yields `H_deriv_pos`. This route reuses the two truncated moments without defining a conditional measure. Do not switch to the double-integral route unless this exact support proof meets the stop condition and the integration owner reopens the decision.

- `mills_upper` compares the lower-tail density integral with `(-x / t) * normalPDF x`; the truncated first-moment identity evaluates the dominating integral exactly.
