## Mathematical role

Normalize each input factor by its positive standard-Gaussian mass. For the normalized factors,
let `Z r` be their correlated product integral after `r` normalized self-convolutions. WP14 gives

```text
Z (r + 1) ≤ Z r ^ 2,
```

so induction yields `Z r ≤ Z 0 ^ (2 ^ r)`. WP16 supplies one fixed positive constant eventually
below `Z r`. If `Z 0 < 1`, the dyadic powers on the right tend to zero, contradicting that lower
bound. Thus `1 ≤ Z 0`; multiplying back the finite product of positive marginal masses proves
`CenteredProductProperty R`.

This package closes M2. It proves only the positive-definite product theorem. Singular covariance
remains an outer orthant-limit problem and does not enter this file. Relevant plan sections are
§§4.2, 4.7–4.8, 6.2, 7, 10, 12 (WP17), 13–14, and 18. The relevant mathematical source section is
§6.3 of `notes/simplex_optimality_proof.v3.md`.

## Proof architecture

1. Normalize `f i` by the inverse of its strictly positive Gaussian mass and prove every
   `CenteredProductProperty` hypothesis for the normalized family.
2. Apply WP14 at every iterate and inductively derive the dyadic-power upper bound.
3. Apply WP16 to obtain a fixed eventual positive lower bound.
4. Compose ordinary geometric decay with `r ↦ 2 ^ r`; contradict the two eventual inequalities if
   the normalized initial integral is below one.
5. Factor the positive product of masses through the correlated integral and denormalize.
