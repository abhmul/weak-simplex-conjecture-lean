## Mathematical role

Define the report's unconstrained `s`-space potential, prove continuity and the symmetric trial bound, show every negative superlevel is compact, and obtain a global maximizer. The acceptance theorem packages the global maximum with the benchmark lower bound consumed by WP07.

Relevant plan sections: §§3.2–3.5, 5.4, 11.3, 12 (WP06), 13, and 17. Relevant mathematical source: §§7–8 of `notes/simplex_optimality_proof.v3.md`, with the reassessment's `s`-space replacement taking precedence over the source's `b`-space formulation.

## Proof architecture

For `w = displacement c s` on a negative superlevel, negativity of each local mass gives `qform R⁻¹ w ≤ -2L`; WP05 coercivity bounds `‖w‖`. The potential is also at most each `localLogMass (s i)`, so the WP04 `atBot` limit gives a common lower coordinate bound. Since `r (s i) > 0`, one has `s i < H (s i) = w i + c`; the coordinate norm bound on `w` gives a common upper bound. Summing the coordinate-square estimates bounds `‖s‖`. Continuity makes the superlevel closed, so finite-dimensional Heine–Borel makes it compact.

For assumption-minimal existence, maximize on a negative compact superlevel containing the origin. For the acceptance theorem, compare that global maximizer with the symmetric trial point and apply `adaptivePotential_trial_ge`.
