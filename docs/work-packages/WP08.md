## Mathematical role

This package closes the change-of-measure and one-dimensional factor edge needed by the conditional
positive-definite orthant theorem. It proves the Gaussian shift for positive-semidefinite, hence
possibly singular, covariance; constructs the centered tilted half-lines; verifies every centered
product hypothesis; and identifies the shifted adaptive endpoint event with the target lower
orthant. It does not invoke the centered-product theorem or prove the orthant comparison.

## Search and prototype record

Pinned mathlib exact-name and semantic searches found no existing multivariate exponential-shift
measure identity. Mathlib supplies the one-dimensional shift theorem
`ProbabilityTheory.gaussianReal_withDensity_exp_shift`, translated Gaussian maps, Gaussian-density
measurability, and with-density integral formulas, but not the required PSD multivariate wrapper.

WP01 compiled and axiom-audited the exact 397-line StatLean closure. A production-shaped prototype
at `/tmp/WP08WrapperPrototype.lean` compiles the two Gaussian wrapper declarations, all frozen
half-line declarations, the zero-barycenter specialization, and the adaptive endpoint preimage
identity. Its three top audits report exactly `[propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound]`.

The prototype established two implementation choices:

- derive the expectation theorem from the measure identity plus translation by `Measure.map`;
- derive mass and first moment by the one-dimensional with-density shift, rather than differentiating
  under the integral sign.
