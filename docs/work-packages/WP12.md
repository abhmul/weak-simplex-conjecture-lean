## Mathematical role

This package lifts WP11's finite-radius symmetric Gaussian rectangle inequality to bounded,
measurable, nonnegative, even log-concave one-dimensional factors. It is the exact even-factor
input used after conditioning on the normalized average in WP14.

Relevant plan sections are §§4.2–4.4, 6.2, 7, 10, 12 (WP12), 13, 14, and 17. The relevant
mathematical source section is Corollary 5.2 in §5 of
`notes/simplex_optimality_proof.v3.md`.

## Proof architecture

1. Classify a convex symmetric subset of `ℝ` as either `univ` or almost everywhere equal under
   `gaussianReal 0 1` to `Icc (-r) r`. Empty sets use `r = 0`; Gaussian no-atoms removes all
   endpoint choices.
2. Approximate `univ` coordinates by `Icc (-n) n`, keep bounded-coordinate radii fixed, apply WP11,
   and pass to the finite-product limit. Lift one-dimensional almost-everywhere equality through
   the standard Gaussian coordinate marginals.
3. Represent a finite product of nonnegative factors as an integral over
   `Measure.pi (fun _ ↦ volume.restrict (Set.Ioi 0))` of coordinatewise strict-superlevel
   events. Prove the identity by finite-product Fubini and Tonelli in ENNReal.
4. At positive levels, log-concavity makes each strict superlevel convex and evenness makes it
   symmetric. Apply the coordinate-set theorem pointwise and integrate the inequality.
5. Use boundedness and nonnegativity to convert the ENNReal result to the frozen real-valued
   acceptance theorem.
